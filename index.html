PhD Research

Filtering of Environmental Effects for Ultrasonic Based Monitoring Using Embedded Piezoelectric Transducers

The research team at ULB's BATir department, led by Prof. Arnaud Deraemaeker, is developing an automated, real-time monitoring system using ultrasonic transducers embedded in concrete — monitoring tunnels around Brussels city. The project addresses challenges where temperature and humidity significantly impact ultrasonic measurements. Methodologies are based on Physics, Statistics and Machine Learning.

  • 1
    Develop innovative damage indicators less sensitive to environmental variations, possibly incorporating non-linear ultrasonic measurements.
  • 2
    Apply statistical methods to filter out environmental and operational influences, identifying and isolating damage-related signal changes.
View USES2 Project →

This research is part of the USES2 (Novel Ultrasonic and Seismic methods) European project, funded by the European Union under the Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions programme.
