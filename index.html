PhD Research Topic

Filtering of Environmental Effects for Ultrasonic Based Monitoring Using Embedded Piezoelectric Transducers

The research team at ULB's BATir department, led by Prof. Arnaud Deraemaeker, is developing an automated, real-time monitoring system using ultrasonic transducers embedded in concrete. The system aims to detect damage early, reducing repair costs and traffic disruptions. Successful laboratory tests have shown promise under controlled conditions. The project addresses field-application challenges where temperature and humidity significantly impact ultrasonic signal measurements, potentially causing false alarms.

  • 1
    Develop innovative damage indicators that are less sensitive to environmental variations, possibly incorporating non-linear ultrasonic measurements.
  • 2
    Apply statistical methods to filter out environmental and operational influences, identifying and isolating damage-related changes.
